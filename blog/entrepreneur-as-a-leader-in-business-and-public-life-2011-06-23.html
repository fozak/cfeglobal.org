Entrepreneur as a Leader in Business and Public Life

June 23, 2011

Annual Conference on Entrepreneurship and Public Life

The Center for Entrepreneurship holds its annual conference "Entrepreneur as a leader in business and public life". The main purpose of the event is to provide entrepreneurs with a platform to discuss effective management of growing companies, share best practices in information technology, personnel management, capital raising, and explore the social contract between businessmen and their environment. The event will be dynamic, addressing business owners aiming for growth, knowledge, new contacts, and social problem-solving. Dr. Ray Smilor, an expert in entrepreneurship, will speak on "How to keep the lead: managing growing companies". Conference participants will discuss the use of information technology for business development and participate in thematic sections on talent acquisition and capital raising. Bruce Poon Tip, founder of Gap Adventures, will deliver a report on "Sustainable development and entrepreneurship". The conference will conclude with a gala evening to recognize partners and associates, followed by networking. Duration: 9:00 - 18:00, Gala: 18:00 - 20:00.

Key Organizations and Contributions

The Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year program celebrates the contributions of entrepreneurial leaders across various industries. Facilitating leadership development and acknowledging the achievements of these leaders, this program is vital for fostering a vibrant business community. Another critical entity is Gap Adventures, founded by Bruce Poon Tip. Known for outstanding business solutions and sustainable practices, Gap Adventures serves as a model for integrating business success with social responsibility. The Fasttrac program empowers entrepreneurs with the tools and strategies necessary to sustain business growth. These efforts are critical in promoting sustainable development and supporting the next generation of business leaders.
