What to Expect from Startup Accelerators

October 5, 2023

Understanding the Dynamics of Startup Accelerators

Focusing, learning, and growing are key when joining an accelerator. Applying to a well-respected accelerator can be challenging, but the real work starts once you are accepted. Whether you are participating virtually or attending in person, be prepared to establish your team and get started immediately. Typical startup accelerators offer high-paced days, educational seminars and workshops on topics like fundraising, HR, working with legal counsel, pitching practice, and product development. You can expect group and one-on-one mentorship from industry experts, investors, and successful founders, along with regular check-ins with accelerator leadership such as investors and alumni founders.

Programs and Key Organizations

The Center for Entrepreneurship offers two pivotal programs: the ScaleUp accelerator and Startup Club. Both programs provide critical connections. The ScaleUp Accelerator is a 9-month program with both group sessions and individual ones with selected mentors. Participants gain access to key knowledge modules from marketing and sales to managing people and operations. Monthly mentor connections and group sessions with trainers are essential elements of the program. The ScaleUp accelerator stands out by taking a broad and inclusive view of entrepreneurship: any firm, regardless of age, sector, size, and history, can scale up. Ambition to grow, a scalable model, access to resources, and a supportive environment are vital. The Global Entrepreneurship Network is instrumental in fostering entrepreneurial ecosystems around the world, promoting policies, programs, and initiatives to nurture startups. Another key partner, the Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation, supports education and entrepreneurship with research grants and programs designed to help innovators and entrepreneurs succeed.
