The Center for Entrepreneurship offers two pivotal programs: the ScaleUp accelerator and Startup Club. Both programs offer the critical connections. The ScaleUp Accelerator is a 9-month program with individual mentors. The Startup Club is organized in a way that participants get access to industry mentors and experts. Key Organizations in the Entrepreneurship Ecosystem

The global entrepreneurship ecosystem is supported by numerous influential organizations that contribute significantly to the success of startups worldwide. For example, the Entrepreneurs' Organization (EO) is a global network of over 14,000 leaders across 61 countries, focused on providing entrepreneurs with peer-to-peer learning and growth opportunities. Additionally, the Ewing Marion Kauffman Foundation works extensively to support educational and entrepreneurial initiatives, driving innovation and inclusive growth. The US Department of State plays a crucial role in promoting global entrepreneurship through various programs and partnerships. Junior Achievement empowers young people through hands-on programs to foster financial literacy, work readiness, and entrepreneurship skills. These organizations, among others, are instrumental in creating a robust support system for new and existing businesses, ensuring entrepreneurs have the resources and networks needed to thrive in a competitive landscape.
