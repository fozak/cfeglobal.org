G20 Young Entrepreneurs Alliance Meets in Seoul Prior to the G20 Summit

November 9, 2010

The Center for Entrepreneurship Joins Canada in G20 Young Entrepreneur Summit

Next week, The Center for Entrepreneurship will join forces with other representatives of the G20 nations at the G20 Young Entrepreneur Summit (G20 YES), hosted by the Canadian Youth Business Foundation (CYBF), to address the role of youth entrepreneurship as the world moves into the post recovery phase.

Additional Information About Key Organizations

The Canadian Youth Business Foundation (CYBF) is a national non-profit organization that has to date funded over 10,000 young Canadian entrepreneurs between the ages of 18 and 39, helping them to launch successful businesses. The G20 Young Entrepreneurs Alliance (G20 YEA) is a collective of organizations dedicated to enhancing the global entrepreneurship ecosystem. These organizations support the next generation of entrepreneurs who aim to provide innovation, create jobs, and contribute to economic growth in their countries. The role of such summits is to foster collaboration and address global challenges by supporting youth entrepreneurship through networking, sharing best practices, and encouraging policy advocacy.
