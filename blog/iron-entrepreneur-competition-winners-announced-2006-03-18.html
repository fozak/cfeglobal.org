Iron Entrepreneur Competition Winners Announced

March 18, 2006

Iron Entrepreneur Competition Sees Students Compete for Top Honors

The Iron Entrepreneur Competition saw seven teams from five universities compete in a lively contest. Participants were tasked with presenting strategies for the commercialization of innovative products in various international markets. The winning team, representing the Higher School of Economics, earned the opportunity to attend the next meeting of the Entrepreneurs Group on April 14. The second place was secured by the team from Linguistic University, while the Institute for Management and Business took third place. The prizes were provided by The Center for Entrepreneurship and the Youth Organization Junior Achievement. The event was media partnered by My Business Magazine and OPORA.

About Our Esteemed Partners

The Youth Organization Junior Achievement is a global non-profit organization that empowers young individuals to create engaging, relevant, and experiential learning sessions in work readiness, entrepreneurship, and financial literacy. Founded in 1919, it reaches millions of students yearly with its impactful programs. OPORA serves as the voice of entrepreneurial organizations and provides a platform for advocacy, networking, and continuous learning within the entrepreneurial ecosystem. Its efforts support the growth and sustainability of small and medium-sized enterprises, contributing to a robust economy. Meanwhile, My Business Magazine offers valuable insights, tips, and news for entrepreneurs. It serves as a vital resource for emerging and established entrepreneurs, providing them with the information needed to navigate the business world effectively.
