The Center for Entrepreneurship's Management and Staff Overview

July 20, 2003

Meet the Key Team Members at The Center for Entrepreneurship

Eric Hansen is The Center for Entrepreneurship’s Program Manager. He first began working in 1992 and brings to CFE extensive international marketing management and communications experience in the fields of media, high technology, and in training and organizational development. Fluent in Russian, he earned his MBA from Thunderbird, The American Graduate School of International Management, and his BA in Central and Eastern European Studies from the University of Colorado, Boulder. Alla Danilova is Project Coordinator for The Center for Entrepreneurship. Prior to CFE, she coordinated technical assistance and training programs, translated and interpreted at international events for OECD, and was resident advisor’s assistant for the U.S. Department of Commerce's program on WTO accession. Fluent in English, she earned her BA in Foreign Languages at the Moscow Linguistic University.

About the Organizations

OECD, or the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development, is an international organization focused on promoting policies that improve the economic and social well-being of people around the world. Established in 1961, it provides a forum where governments can work together to share experiences and seek solutions to common problems. The U.S. Department of Commerce is a cabinet-level agency of the U.S. federal government tasked with promoting economic growth. The mission of the Department is to create the conditions for economic growth and opportunity, with functions in trade, economic development, technology, entrepreneurship, and statistical research.
