September 25, 2006

Center for Entrepreneurship announces new board members

The Center for Entrepreneurship has announced its new board members. The board comprises prominent figures from various sectors who bring a wealth of experience and knowledge. The new members of the board are expected to focus on advancing the center's mission of fostering entrepreneurship and supporting innovative business ventures.

The new board members include:

  • Richard G. Turner, Chairman - Former Executive Vice President, South Shore Bank.
  • Jenne K. Britell - Member, Board of Directors, the U.S. Russia Investment Fund. Chairman and CEO, Structured Ventures, Inc.
  • Steven L. Pease - Member, Board of Directors, the U.S. Russia Investment Fund. General Partner, Deucalion Securities.

Richard G. Turner: A Leader in Community Development

Richard G. Turner has made significant contributions to the field of community development throughout his career. As the former Executive Vice President of South Shore Bank, he played a key role in implementing programs aimed at economic empowerment and financial inclusion. Under his leadership, the bank pioneered several initiatives that provided crucial support to small and medium enterprises, enabling growth and fostering entrepreneurship.

Turner's extensive background in finance and community banking made him a suitable choice for the role of Chairman at the Center for Entrepreneurship. His vision aligns with the center's goals to nurture entrepreneurial talent and provide necessary resources for business innovation. His influence is expected to drive forward the center's strategic initiatives and create new opportunities for aspiring entrepreneurs.

Beyond his professional achievements, Turner is also known for his dedication to volunteerism and social causes. He has been actively involved in various philanthropic efforts, contributing both time and resources to make a positive impact on local communities. His holistic approach to leadership and community service underscores his commitment to fostering a thriving entrepreneurial ecosystem.
