The Center for Entrepreneurship Partners with My Business Magazine

April 15, 2003

Exciting New Partnerships and Initiatives for The Center for Entrepreneurship

The Center for Entrepreneurship has entered into several exciting partnerships and initiatives aimed at fostering entrepreneurial growth and providing valuable resources to young business owners. On April 10, 2003, My Business Magazine signed a partnership and cooperation agreement to become the general media partner, ensuring comprehensive informational support for the Center's educational and networking initiatives. In addition, an agreement was signed with OPORA, a non-governmental organization supporting small and medium businesses, to further enhance entrepreneurial opportunities. Moreover, the Young Entrepreneurs Organization (YEO) opened its first chapter thanks to this collaboration. This chapter offers peer-to-peer advice, business information services, and global connections to select entrepreneurs under 40.

Key Organizations Driving Entrepreneurship

My Business Magazine is a widely recognized publication dedicated to helping entrepreneurs and business owners succeed. By becoming the general media partner for The Center for Entrepreneurship, the magazine will provide extensive coverage and support for various educational and networking programs. The OPORA organization plays a significant role in advocating for small and medium-sized enterprises, ensuring that these businesses have access to the necessary resources and support to thrive. The cooperation with The Center for Entrepreneurship will enable both entities to pool expertise and offer improved services to local entrepreneurs. The Young Entrepreneurs' Organization (YEO) is a global network that aids young entrepreneurs in building connections and accessing valuable business insights. The establishment of its first chapter through this collaboration will provide immense support to emerging business leaders.
