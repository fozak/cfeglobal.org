Center for Entrepreneurship Announces New Managing Organizations and Advisory Board

Washington DC, March 17, 2003

Center for Entrepreneurship Introduces New Leadership

The Center for Entrepreneurship is proud to announce its newly appointed managing organizations, senior management, Board of Directors, Management and Staff, and Advisory Board. This team of esteemed professionals brings a wealth of experience and expertise to the organization, poised to drive forward its mission of fostering entrepreneurial growth and innovation.

Key Personnel

Advisory Board:

  • Blake Marshall - Executive Vice President, US Russia Business Council; President, U.S. Russia Business Forum.
  • Misha Plam - President and CEO, AlphaSniffer, LLC.
  • John Sullivan - Executive Director, Center for International Private Enterprise (CIPE).
  • Carol Vipperman - President, Foundation for Russian American Economic Cooperation (FRAEC).
Highlighted Biography:
John Sullivan, Executive Director of Center for International Private Enterprise (CIPE), brings a remarkable background in promoting private enterprise and democratic governance across the globe. Sullivan has been a stalwart advocate for economic reforms, emphasizing the critical role of institutional integrity and market-oriented policies. He has guided numerous international projects, impacting policy and development at national levels. His involvement has been pivotal in navigating complex economic environments to foster sustainable growth. Through his leadership, CIPE has expanded its reach, delivering impactful results worldwide. His tenure at CIPE is marked by innovative strategies and successful collaborations. For more information, you can visit his [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Sullivan).
