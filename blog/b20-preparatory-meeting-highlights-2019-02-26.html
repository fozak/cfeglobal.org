B20 Preparatory Meeting Highlights

February 26, 2019

Key Outcomes from the B20 Preparatory Meeting

On February 26, 2019, the preparatory meeting for the B20 summit took place in Japan. Organized by the Japan Business Federation (Keidanren), this meeting focused on critical issues such as the development of Industry 5.0 and its implications for global business and entrepreneurship. The B20 summit, traditionally part of the G20, aims to provide recommendations to the G20 leaders and serves as a global business community platform. This year’s discussions centered around the need for advancements in technology and industry to foster sustainable economic growth. Industry 5.0 was discussed extensively, highlighting the shift towards integrated human-machine collaboration. The attendees included high-profile business leaders and government officials who emphasized the necessity of implementing policies that support innovation and entrepreneurship. A significant part of the discussions also revolved around the need for skilled workforce and educational programs tailored to the demands of the future market.

Also at the Meeting

The B20 forum included numerous stakeholders that play pivotal roles in global economic governance and business leadership. Notable among these were (B20) which serves as the G20’s official voice of the private sector. The key topics discussed included the regulatory frameworks needed for fostering entrepreneurship, and how digital transformation can be accelerated across various sectors. The B20 made several recommendations, such as enhancing public-private partnerships and investing in digital infrastructure. Another major highlight was the focus on sustainable development goals, urging businesses to adopt practices that are environmentally friendly and inclusive. Delegates emphasized the need for collaboration among nations to achieve a resilient global economy. The Center for Entrepreneurship and other organizations are expected to play a crucial role in implementing these recommendations on a local level. The meeting concluded with a consensus on the importance of innovation, sustainability, and cohesive policy frameworks to tackle future challenges in the global economy.
