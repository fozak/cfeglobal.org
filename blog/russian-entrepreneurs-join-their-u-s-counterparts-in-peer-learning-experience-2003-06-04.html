Russian Entrepreneurs Join Their U.S. Counterparts in Peer-Learning Experience

June 4, 2003

U.S.-Russia Entrepreneurs Exchange to Foster International Business Collaboration

Recognizing an opportunity for expanded international cooperation and the fact that entrepreneurs learn best from each other, the Foundation for Enterprise Development (FED) and The Center for Entrepreneurship (CFE), in partnership with the Young Entrepreneurs’ Organization (YEO), are hosting the “The U.S. Russia Entrepreneurs Exchange,” a peer-learning program that links Russian and American entrepreneurs, from May 29 through June 6, 2003. The program will take place in five cities across the United States. Six Russian entrepreneurs from fast-growing companies will be matched with U.S.-based business leaders from similar industries. The goal of the Entrepreneurs Exchange is to immerse Russian entrepreneurs in the American entrepreneurial culture, and expose their American hosts to the business culture of Russia. “It’s a way to build relationships between entrepreneurs within a shared industry sector as a foundation for future business opportunities or trade,” said Ray Smilor, president of the Foundation for Enterprise Development. “By sharing ideas and strategies, participants can learn from each other and build better, higher performing organizations.” The program will bring together industry leaders from varied backgrounds, including food, public relations, finance, television production, manufacturing, and pharmaceuticals, providing them with an intensive hands-on experience in their respective fields. Participants are chosen through a competitive application process and then selected by a joint committee of FED and The Center for Entrepreneurship. The U.S. participants will include Ashley Postlewaite, CEO of Renegade Animation, Inc.; Christopher Rosica, CEO of Rosica Mulhern, Inc.; Joe Polo, CEO of Original Juan; Jonathan Slater, CEO of Sequel Management; James Weldon, CEO of MaxDisplays.com; and Monique Muri, CEO of Ram Scientific Corp.

About Key Organizations

The Foundation for Enterprise Development is a non-profit organization dedicated to advancing entrepreneurship through education, training, and consulting. Established to cultivate entrepreneurial growth worldwide, it supports various initiatives and collaborations aimed at fostering a thriving business ecosystem. The Young Entrepreneurs’ Organization (YEO) is a global network of young business leaders, providing peer-to-peer mentorship, business resources, and networking opportunities. Renowned for its innovative approach to supporting under-40 entrepreneurs, YEO facilitates valuable interactions and learning experiences among its members to drive entrepreneurial success.
