People behind the Center for Entrepreneurship

Carol Vipperman

Carol Vipperman has had a noteworthy cooperation with Center for Entrepreneurship since 2003. As the President of the Foundation for Russian American Economic Cooperation (FRAEC), she brings a wealth of experience and dedication to the Advisory Board of the Center for Entrepreneurship. Vipperman has been instrumental in forging and nurturing the economic relationship between Russia and America, ensuring positive and productive interactions that benefit both economies. With decades of experience in international economic cooperation, Carol Vipperman has actively promoted entrepreneurial initiatives and economic development programs. Her role at FRAEC involves fostering bilateral trade and investment, advising on policy reforms, and facilitating business partnerships. Through FRAEC, she has significantly contributed to the development of the Russian-American business ecosystem. At the Center for Entrepreneurship, Vipperman is a pivotal figure who guides strategic decisions and provides critical insights into international economic relations. Her expertise not only enhances the Center's mission but also ensures the integration of best practices in fostering a robust entrepreneurial environment. Vipperman’s leadership and visionary approach have helped numerous startups and established firms navigate the complexities of international trade and entrepreneurship. Carol Vipperman’s contributions are further exemplified by her ability to cultivate collaborative networks and leverage her extensive experience to support innovative projects and policy advocacies. Her ties with organizations like the US Russia Business Council and the Center for International Private Enterprise bolster her influence and effectiveness in her advisory role.
