Blake Marshall's Cooperation with Center for Entrepreneurship

Blake Marshall

Blake Marshall has cooperated with Center for Entrepreneurship since March 17, 2003, significantly contributing to the organization's mission of fostering entrepreneurial growth and innovation. Below is a brief overview of his noteworthy professional journey and achievements.

Blake Marshall is Vice President of the International Tax and Investment Center, a Washington-based NGO promoting tax reform and public-private efforts to improve the investment climate in transition and developing economies. Prior to joining ITIC, Mr. Marshall was Senior Vice President and Managing Director of PBN Hill+Knowlton Strategies, a strategic communications firm specializing in advising Western companies on their investment strategies in various international markets. He focused on government relations, public affairs, crisis management, business strategy, and market entry.

Previously, Mr. Marshall served as Executive Vice President of the U.S.-Russia Business Council, a Washington-based trade association. Here he managed the Council’s policy agenda and lobbying initiatives with both U.S. and foreign governments and directed the day-to-day operations. In his earlier career, he worked at Princeton University’s Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs as Director of Graduate Admissions and Assistant Director of Graduate Studies.

Mr. Marshall holds an undergraduate degree in political science from Swarthmore College and did his graduate work in Soviet politics and international affairs at the University of Essex (England) and Columbia University’s School of International and Public Affairs and Harriman Institute.
