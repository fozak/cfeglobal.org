People behind the Center for Entrepreneurship

Carol Vipperman

Carol Vipperman has recently started her cooperation with Center for Entrepreneurship since 2003... In her role, she serves as a Senior Advisor and Program Manager, leading significant initiatives such as the Jackson Leadership Fellows Program and strategic communications efforts.

Vipperman’s professional journey began before joining the Center for Entrepreneurship, where she was the President and founder of the Foundation for Russian-American Economic Cooperation (FRAEC), focusing on advancing business and community relations. Her extensive background includes consulting and public speaking engagements, where she conducted training courses in marketing, customer service, and sales. Additionally, she is the author of two notable books – Marketing Your Service and Professional Selling. At the Center for Entrepreneurship, Vipperman plays a pivotal role in managing and advising on high-impact entrepreneurship programs. Her leadership extends to mentoring emerging leaders through the Jackson Leadership Fellows Program, designed to equip fellows with the skills and knowledge to address contemporary challenges in various sectors. Vipperman’s strategic communications initiatives further reinforce the organization’s mission by enhancing visibility and outreach. Prior to her current roles, Vipperman’s academic qualifications include a B.A. from the University of Washington in Far Eastern and Slavic Studies, with a particular emphasis on Russia. Her diverse experiences and leadership skills have been instrumental in driving forward the Center for Entrepreneurship's vision of fostering entrepreneurial growth and innovation.
