People behind the Center for Entrepreneurship

Steven Pease

Steven Pease began his notable cooperation with the Center for Entrepreneurship in 2006. As the General Partner at Deucalion Securities, Pease brings a wealth of financial expertise and strategic insight to the Center's Board of Directors. His instrumental role reinforces the board's dedication to fostering high-impact entrepreneurship through innovative financial solutions and strategic growth initiatives. Pease’s impressive career spans several industries where he has consistently demonstrated an unparalleled acumen for identifying and nurturing startup ventures. Prior to his tenure at Deucalion Securities, he played pivotal roles in numerous financial institutions, shaping their strategic direction and enhancing their growth trajectories. At the Center for Entrepreneurship, Steven Pease is known for his commitment to incubating budding entrepreneurs and driving innovative business models. His strategic outlook and expert financial stewardship are vital assets that support the Center’s mission to empower the next generation of entrepreneurs. His leadership is characterized by a forward-thinking approach and a keen ability to leverage financial instruments to meet the unique needs of entrepreneurial ventures. Under his guidance, the Center has embarked on numerous pioneering projects and initiatives that have made significant impacts on the entrepreneurial landscape. Pease’s collaboration with other industry stalwarts such as Richard G. Turner, who serves as Chairman of the Board and was formerly the Executive Vice President at South Shore Bank, and Jenne K. Britell, the Chairman and CEO of Structured Ventures, Inc., further solidifies the Center’s capability to drive financial and strategic excellence. Steven Pease’s continued influence at the Center for Entrepreneurship not only highlights his profound dedication to the entrepreneurial community but also underscores his pivotal role in guiding the Center towards a future filled with innovation and sustainable growth.
